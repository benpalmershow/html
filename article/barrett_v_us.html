Understanding Double Jeopardy: Tools and Timeline

Interactive reference for Barrett v. United States and the doctrines shaping cumulative punishment analysis.

Conviction vs. Punishment

Conviction: The Blockburger elements test asks whether each offense requires proof of a fact the other does not. If not, they are the same offense for double-jeopardy purposes. The focus is on elements, not on punishment.

From Blockburger to Barrett

1932

Blockburger v. United States

Established the “same-elements” test: two offenses are distinct if each requires proof of a fact the other does not.

1983

Whalen v. United States

Held that cumulative punishment requires a clear indication of congressional intent.

2017

Dean v. United States

Recognized judicial discretion in sentencing alongside §924(c) mandatory minimums.

2023

United States v. Lora

Clarified that §924(j) sentences are not subject to §924(c)’s consecutive-sentence rule, signaling separate statutory design.

2025

Barrett v. United States

Tests whether §924(j) subsumes §924(c) and whether double punishment violates the Fifth Amendment’s double-jeopardy clause.
