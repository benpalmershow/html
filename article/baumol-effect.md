# The Baumol Effect

A string quartet playing Beethoven in 2026 needs the same four musicians for the same thirty minutes as in 1966. Output is flat. But those musicians aren't earning 1966 wages.

**Wages rise everywhere. Even in jobs where output per hour has not changed.**

**Manufacturing / tech:** A worker with robots builds more cars than five assembly-line workers in 1970. Productivity rises, wages rise, prices fall.

**Healthcare, education, law, arts:** A nurse changes a bandage, a professor grades an essay — same time in 2026 as 1966. Productivity doesn't budge. **But wages still rise** — because if a nurse can make more at Amazon, she leaves. Costs climb — without a productivity gain to justify it.
