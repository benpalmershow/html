### <i data-lucide='users' class='post-icon'></i> Jobs: Down 105k, up 64k MoM

Total nonfarm payroll employment rose to 159,552,000 in November, adding 64,000 jobs from October's 159,488,000. Job gains remain solid but moderate, consistent with a cooling labor market. The unemployment rate continues to edge higher as slack builds into the labor force.
- **November**: 159,552,000 (+64,000 MoM)
- **October**: 159,488,000 (-105,000 MoM)
- **September**: 159,593,000 (+108,000 MoM)

{{chart:Total Nonfarm Employment}}

The S&P Global Composite PMI (Flash) declined to 53.0 in December from November's 54.2, signaling slower private sector expansion. A reading above 50 still indicates growth, but the downward trend reflects cooling momentum heading into the new year. Manufacturing and services both contributing to the slowdown.
