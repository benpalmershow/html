### <i data-lucide='shopping-cart' class='post-icon'></i> **Consumer Confidence Down**

Consumer confidence fell sharply in December to 89.1, down 3.8 points (-4.1%) from November's 92.9.
- **December**: 89.1 (-3.8 points, -4.1% MoM)
- **November**: 92.9
- **3-Month Trend**: Declining from 95.5 (October)

The Conference Board's survey of 3,000+ households reveals weakening consumer sentiment across expectations and current conditions. This drop underscores consumer anxiety about employment, inflation, and overall economic stability despite recent market strength.

**Sentiment vs. Confidence**

While confidence fell, [sentiment rose to 52.9](financials.html?filter=Consumer%20Indicators) in December (up from 51 in November). The two surveys measure different aspects: UMich focuses on current personal finances and future buying intentions, while Conference Board tracks expectations about employment and general business conditions. The Sentiment uptick likely reflects relief from lower inflation and stable housing markets, while the Confidence decline suggests erosion in job security outlook and economic growth expectations as the year closes.
