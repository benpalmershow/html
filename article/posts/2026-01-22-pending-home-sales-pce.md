### <i data-lucide='home' class='post-icon'></i> **Pending Home Sales Index Down**

December pending home sales dropped to 71.8, a 9.3% decline from November's 79.2. The index remains well below long-term averages, signaling continued weakness in the housing market despite lower mortgage rates.

{{chart:Pending Home Sales Index}}

The decline underscores softening housing demand as homebuyers navigate affordability constraints and seasonal slowdowns heading into year-end. As a leading indicator, this weakness typically precedes slower existing home sales in the coming weeks.

### <i data-lucide='shopping-cart' class='post-icon'></i> **Consumer Spending Remains Steady**

Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) rose to $21,409.7B in November, up 0.5% from October's $21,301.0B. While this data trails December's pending home sales report, consumer spending continues to support economic growth despite headwinds in housing and tightening labor markets.

{{chart:Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE)}}

The modest increase reflects consumers' persistent purchasing, though growth rates remain below pre-pandemic trends. This resilience suggests consumers are still engaging the economy even as housing activity cools, bolstering the case for a soft landing scenario.
