**Consumer Sentiment vs. Confidence - Mixed Signals:**

Consumer sentiment improved notably in January, rising to 56.4 from December's 52.9 - a 3.5-point gain suggesting better household optimism. However, The Conference Board's Consumer Confidence Index painted a more pessimistic picture, plummeting to 84.5 from December's 94.2 (-10.3% MoM). The divergence suggests near-term sentiment is brightening while longer-term expectations are souring.
