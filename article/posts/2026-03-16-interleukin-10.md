### <i data-lucide='atom' class='post-icon'></i> **Interleukin-10**

IL-10 is a small protein your immune system produces to calm inflammation. When you get injured, your body floods the area with pro-inflammatory signals to fight infection and start healing. IL-10 is the off switch. It tells immune cells to stand down once the job is done.

A February 2026 study in *Science Immunology* from Michigan State found that IL-10 is also the reason men resolve pain faster than women. Monocytes (a type of white blood cell) produce IL-10 and signal directly to pain-sensing neurons to shut them off. Male monocytes produce significantly more IL-10 than female monocytes because testosterone drives production. When researchers blocked male sex hormones, the advantage disappeared.

The same pattern showed up in human data. Researchers at UNC Chapel Hill tracked patients after car accidents and found men had higher circulating IL-10 levels and resolved pain faster. Women experienced longer-lasting pain, not because of perception or reporting differences, but because their immune systems produced less of the molecule that actively resolves it.

"The difference in pain between men and women has a biological basis. It's not in your head, and you're not soft. It's in your immune system." Geoffroy Laumet, MSU.

Mice without IL-10 develop lethal gut inflammation under normal conditions. The molecule also enhances B cell function and antibody production. Researchers are now exploring whether treatments can boost IL-10 production to resolve pain faster without opioids.
