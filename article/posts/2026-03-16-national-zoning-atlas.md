### <i data-lucide='map' class='post-icon'></i> **National Zoning Atlas**

The National Zoning Atlas has digitized zoning codes across 9,200+ jurisdictions covering 185 million people, reading over 1 million pages of zoning regulations to build a searchable, interactive map of how American land can be used.

The findings are striking. Most residential land in the country is zoned exclusively for single-family homes. Minimum lot sizes, parking requirements, and density restrictions lock out duplexes, triplexes, and small apartment buildings from the vast majority of buildable land. In Connecticut, 76% of single-family land requires minimum lot sizes of 80,000 square feet or more. In New Hampshire, only 5.6% of buildable land has access to both water and sewer services.

The atlas is already driving policy. Colorado passed legislation eliminating minimum lot sizes over 2,000 square feet for single-family homes, citing NZA data. Montana used its state atlas to pass bipartisan statewide land use reforms. Salem, Massachusetts eliminated parking requirements for new apartments after reviewing the map.

Founded by Sara Bronin, the project won the 30th Heinz Award for the Economy in 2025.
