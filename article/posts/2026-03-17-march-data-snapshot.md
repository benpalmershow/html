<i data-lucide='chart-line' class='post-icon'></i> <b>March Data Snapshot</b>

<p>Oil spiking, housing bouncing, the Fed sitting on its hands, and recession odds climbing. Here's where the numbers stand.</p>

**WTI crude** jumped to $91.27 in March from $64.51 in February. That's a 41.5% move in one month. The Iran conflict and Hormuz disruptions are doing what they do. Polymarket puts 79% odds on oil hitting $100 by end of March.

{{chart:WTI Crude Oil}}

**Existing home sales** rebounded to 4.09M in February after falling to 3.91M in January. Still below December's 4.27M. Buyers are testing the water but rates keep the lid on.

{{chart:Existing Home Sales}}

The **FOMC meets tomorrow**. 99% chance they hold. Nobody expects a cut. Nobody expects a hike. The Fed watches oil and waits.

**Recession odds** sit at 31%, up from 23% in January. Tech layoffs market at 87% yes. The labor market is softening while energy costs spike. That combination has a name.

**Senate midterms** are a coin flip. Democrats 51¢, Republicans 50¢. The referendum on Trump's second term starts now.
