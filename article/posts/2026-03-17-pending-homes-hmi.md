<i data-lucide='home'></i> <b>Pending Home Sales Index and Housing Market Index</b>

<p>Pending sales ticked up in February while builder confidence edged higher in March. Small moves, same story.</p>

The **Pending Home Sales Index** rose to 72.1 in February from 70.8 in January, a 1.8% increase. That reverses part of December's slide from 77.2 to 71.5 but stays well below fall levels. Buyers are testing the water. Rates keep the ceiling low.

{{chart:Pending Home Sales Index}}

The **NAHB Housing Market Index** came in at 38 for March, up from 37 in February. Builder confidence has flatlined in the mid-to-high 30s since last summer. Anything below 50 means more builders see conditions as poor than good. We have been there for over a year.

{{chart:Housing Market Index}}

Rates, inventory, and affordability remain the three walls closing in. Nothing in either report suggests a breakout.
