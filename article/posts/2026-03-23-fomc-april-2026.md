**Market Context:**

The March FOMC meeting resulted in a hold as expected, with the Fed maintaining its patient stance amid mixed economic signals. Oil prices have surged 41% year-to-date, with WTI crude now above $90/barrel - a development the Fed is closely monitoring for potential inflationary pressure.

Recession odds have climbed to 31%, up from 23% at the start of the year. Despite softening labor market indicators, the Fed appears positioned to remain on hold through the April meeting, awaiting more clarity on the inflation trajectory and the impact of energy price increases on consumer spending.

The next rate cut is not expected until later in 2026, with prediction markets pricing in a 25 basis point cut by the June or July meeting at roughly 55-60% probability.
