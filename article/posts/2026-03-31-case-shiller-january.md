### <i data-lucide='home' class='post-icon'></i> **Case-Shiller Decline Continues into 2026**

The Case-Shiller National Home Price Index dipped to 326.612 in January, down 0.1% from December's 326.969. The index has been drifting lower since peaking at 331.608 last June, shedding roughly 1.5% over seven months.

{{chart:Case-Shiller National Home Price Index}}

Home prices are not crashing, but they are no longer rising. Affordability constraints from mortgage rates above 6% have capped buyer demand while inventory has slowly rebuilt. The result is a market where sellers still list high but eventually cut, and buyers pick through stale listings looking for motivated counterparts.

Year-over-year, January's reading is down 0.3% from 327.752 last March. The annual gains that characterized the post-pandemic era have flipped negative. Whether this stabilizes or accelerates depends on rates, and rates depend on the Fed, and the Fed is watching the same labor market data everyone else is.
