### <i data-lucide='scale' class='post-icon'></i> **One Bad Taste**

OneTaste founder Nicole Daedone was sentenced to nine years in prison and former Head of Sales Rachel Cherwitz to 78 months for forced labor conspiracy (E.D.N.Y., 23-CR-146). Judge Gujarati also imposed a $12 million forfeiture judgment against Daedone and awarded $887,877.64 in restitution to seven victims. Both were convicted by a federal jury in June 2025.

<a href="https://www.justice.gov/usao-edny/pr/onetaste-founder-nicole-daedone-sentenced-nine-years-prison-forced-labor-conspiracy" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">DOJ press release</a> · <a href="https://www.courthousenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/government-opposition-daedone-cherwitz-rule-29-eastern-district-new-york.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Govt Rule 29 opposition (PDF)</a>

The government had filed opposition to the defendants' Rule 29 motion for acquittal, arguing it was procedurally improper and that the First Amendment does not shield criminal conduct carried out in spiritual garb. Nine victim-witnesses testified to patterns of psychological, financial, sexual, and physical coercion spanning 2006 through 2018, including being coerced into becoming sexual "handlers" for OneTaste's initial investor and performing sex acts with prospective clients under threat of termination, demotion, and financial ruin.

U.S. Attorney Nocella stated: "Coercion disguised as wellness or empowerment is still exploitation and it is a crime that causes harm to vulnerable victims." FBI ADIC Barnacle added that Daedone and Cherwitz "preyed on vulnerable women, coercing them into a calculated forced labor conspiracy" through "repeated psychological manipulation and sexual abuse."
