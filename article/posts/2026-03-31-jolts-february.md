### <i data-lucide='briefcase' class='post-icon'></i> **Job Openings down 358,000**

February job openings dropped to 6.88 million, down 358,000 from January's revised 7.24 million. January was revised up from an initial 7.18 million, making the month-over-month decline look steeper than it might have otherwise. Still, the 4.9% monthly drop signals the labor market remains stuck in a tightening-to-loosening transition that started last fall.

The nonfarm side tells a similar story. February nonfarm employment came in at 158.466 million, down 92,000 from January's revised 158.558 million.

{{chart:Job Openings}}

The JOLTS data has been noisy month to month, but the trend is unmistakable. Openings peaked near 7.5 million in 2022 and have stair-stepped lower since. February's print sits near the bottom of the post-pandemic range, well below the 7+ million readings that characterized a tight labor market.

What the data does not show is the other side of the equation. Hiring remains tepid, layoffs have not surged, and the quit rate has stayed subdued for over a year. Workers are holding on. Employers are holding off. The result is a labor market that is neither hot nor cold but frozen in place.
