Judge Richard Leon granted a preliminary injunction today blocking all physical development of the proposed White House ballroom at the former site of the East Wing. The order in *National Trust for Historic Preservation v. National Park Service* (D.D.C., 25-4316) bars demolition, site preparation, excavation, foundation work, and any other construction activity unless strictly necessary for safety and security of the White House grounds.

The 35-page opinion traces White House construction authority back to the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Residence_Act" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Residence Act of 1790</a>, which authorized commissioners to "provide suitable buildings for the accommodation of ... the President." Judge Leon wrote: "The President of the United States is the steward of the White House for future generations of First Families. He is not, however, the owner!" The court found that 3 U.S.C. § 105(d)(1), which authorizes funds for "care, maintenance, repair, alteration, refurnishing, improvement, air-conditioning, heating, and lighting," covers ordinary upkeep — not wholesale demolition and new construction of a $400 million ballroom funded by private donations routed through a "convoluted funding scheme" resembling a "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rube_Goldberg_machine" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Rube Goldberg contraption</a>."

In plain terms: the President is the tenant, Congress is the landlord. You can't gut the kitchen and build an addition without asking first.

The injunction takes effect 14 days from today. Defendants named in the order include the National Park Service, Department of the Interior under Secretary Douglas Burgum, General Services Administration, the Executive Office of the President, White House Chief of Staff Susie Wiles, and the Office of the Executive Residence. The Court further ordered that no such work shall proceed absent express authorization from Congress. Defendants must file a compliance status report within 21 days after the order takes effect.
