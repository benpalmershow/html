### <i data-lucide='landmark' class='post-icon'></i> **FY 2027 Budget: Defense Surges, Everything Else Shrinks**

The White House released its Fiscal Year 2027 budget request today, a "skinny budget" that signals a massive shift in federal priorities. The proposal calls for a staggering $1.5 trillion in total defense funding, while stripping 10% from non-defense discretionary spending to target what the administration calls "woke and wasteful" programs.

The request serves as the opening salvo in the 2027 appropriations cycle, setting the stage for a contentious battle in Congress over the nation's fiscal direction.

#### Key Highlights:

*   **Defense Spending:** A $1.5 trillion total request, representing a major year-over-year rise. This includes a $251 billion increase in base defense discretionary spending and $350 billion in a new reconciliation bill.
*   **Non-Defense Discretionary:** A $73 billion (10%) reduction in base spending, targeting programs the administration describes as "weaponized and wasteful."
*   **Energy and Environment:** Substantial cuts to climate and environmental programs, including $15.2 billion from Department of Energy programs and reductions in EPA environmental justice grants.
*   **NASA:** A 23% reduction to NASA’s budget (totaling $18.8 billion). The request maintains support for Artemis lunar exploration but calls for terminating over 40 missions and eliminating STEM engagement programs.

The proposal reflects a hard-line fiscal conservative approach, prioritizing military strength and border security while aggressively scaling back the administrative state and green energy initiatives.
