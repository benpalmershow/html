### <i data-lucide='trending-up' class='post-icon'></i> **The Shrinking Middle Class Myth**

A new AEI report challenges the populist narrative that America's middle class is hollowing out. Using an **absolute** definition (constant purchasing power thresholds) rather than Pew's relative approach, the data tells a different story.

The "core" middle class did shrink slightly: from 36% of families in 1979 to 31% in 2024. But here's what the doom-sayers miss: the upper-middle class **tripled** from 10% to 31%. Meanwhile, those below the middle class fell from 54% to 35%.

In 2024, for the first time in US history, more families were above the core middle class (35%) than below it (34%). Combined, the lower-, core, and upper-middle classes now represent 78% of families, up from 70% in 1979.

The upper-middle class now receives half of all family income. The authors argue that declining middle-class shares reflect people moving **up**, not down. The pie grew, and everyone got a bigger slice.

---

The lesson: be skeptical of any claim that everyone's getting poorer while the rich get richer. Both can be true. But if the poor are getting richer too, that's not a crisis.
