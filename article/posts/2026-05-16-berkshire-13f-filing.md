## Berkshire Hathaway's Q1 2026 13F Filing

Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway filed its Q1 2026 13F on Friday, revealing a $263B portfolio with 90 positions.

Top holdings:
- Occidental Petroleum ($17.2B)
- Chubb ($11.2B)
- Kraft Heinz ($7.3B)
- Delta Air Lines ($2.6B)
- DaVita ($2.3B)

The filing shows continued focus on energy and insurance, with OXY and Chevron making up nearly 7% of the portfolio combined.

Notably, Berkshire reduced its position in Apple by 15% during the quarter, reflecting Buffett's ongoing caution about tech valuations. The conglomerate also initiated a new position in HP Inc., signaling interest in the personal computing sector.

In other moves, Berkshire exited its stake in Wells Fargo completely, ending a long-held investment that dates back to the 1980s. The sale aligns with Buffett's recent comments about the challenges facing traditional banks in a low-interest-rate environment.

Overall, the 13F filing underscores Berkshire's shift toward tangible assets and cash-generative businesses, while maintaining a substantial cash reserve of $189B for future opportunities.
